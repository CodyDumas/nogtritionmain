Macronutrient Reference
Every gram of each macronutrient provides a fixed number of calories. Understanding this helps you hit your targets accurately.
Protein
Builds and repairs muscle tissue. Essential for recovery and body composition. Recommended intake for athletes is 0.7–1g per lb of bodyweight.
4
kcal/g
Quick reference
25g = 100 kcal 50g = 200 kcal 100g = 400 kcal
Carbohydrates
Primary fuel source for high-intensity training. Glycogen stored in muscle drives performance. Timing around workouts maximises use and minimises fat storage.
4
kcal/g
Quick reference
25g = 100 kcal 50g = 200 kcal 100g = 400 kcal
Fat
Supports hormone production (including testosterone), joint health, and fat-soluble vitamin absorption. Despite being calorie-dense, dietary fat does not directly cause fat gain — total calorie surplus does.
9
kcal/g
Quick reference
11g = 100 kcal 22g = 200 kcal 44g = 400 kcal
Alcohol
Not a macronutrient but calorie-dense. Alcohol suppresses fat oxidation — meaning your body prioritises burning alcohol over stored fat. Not tracked in most apps but contributes to total intake.
